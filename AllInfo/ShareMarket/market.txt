Share Market Definitions:-

1. CAGR :- Rate per year. 
Eg. Infy has 10% gain per year

2. P/E ratio :- It shows how expensive share is. Eg infy has 10:1 pe. 
It means one is paying 100 more than value of share but accenture has 20:1 so it is very
expensive compare to infy means more confident also.

3. Debt to equity ratio :- Company taking debt compare to self value of share. 
Means if ratio is 2:1 then company has taken 2x loan + 1x self value= total investment. 
Higher value means if company's returns are not good then it will downfall very badly inshort "RISKY"

4. PAT :- Profit after tax. Higher PAT is better obvio.

5. ROE :- Return on equity. 
Total profit gain on total assets is ROE. 
Higher the ROE better is performance.

6. P/B ratio :- The price-to-book ratio compares a company's market value to its book value. 
The market value of a company is its share price multiplied by the number of outstanding shares. 
The book value is the net assets of a company. ... Overvalued growth stocks frequently show a combination of low ROE and high P/B ratios

7. EPS :- Earning per share ie
total profit / total no. of shares. 
Higher eps better performnce
