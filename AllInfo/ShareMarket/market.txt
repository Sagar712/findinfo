        <pre style="color: white;"><!--------------------------------------------------------><div><b style=""><font size="5">Share Market Definitions:-</font></b><font size="4">

1. <b><font color="#fdff70">CAGR</font></b> :- Rate per year. 
 Eg. Infy has 10% gain per year

2.<font color="#fdff70"><b> P/E ratio</b></font> :- It shows how expensive share is. Eg infy has 10:1 pe. It means one is paying 100 more than value of share but accenture has 20:1 so it is very
expensive compare to infy means more confident also.

3. <b><font color="#fdff70">Debt to equity ratio</font></b> :- company taking debt compare to self value of share. Means if ratio is 2:1 then company has taken 2x loan + 1x self value= total
investment. Higher value means if company's returns are not good then it will downfall very badly inshort "RISKY"

4. <b><font color="#fdff70">PAT</font></b> :- profit after tax. Higher PAT is better obvio.

5. <b><font color="#fdff70">ROE</font></b> :- return on equity. Total profit gain on total assets is ROE. Higher the ROE better is performance.

6. <b><font color="#fdff70">P/B ratio</font></b> :- The price-to-book ratio compares a company's market value to its book value. The market value of a company is its share price multiplied by the
number of outstanding shares. The book value is the net assets of a company. ... Overvalued growth stocks frequently show a combination of low ROE and
high P/B ratios

7. <b><font color="#fdff70">EPS</font></b> :- Earning per share ie
total profit / total no. of shares. Higher eps better performance
